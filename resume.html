Here is a bit about me

Background

My name is Kári Genevois. I'm Icelandic on my mother's side and french on my father's. I grew up in France near Paris and decided to move to Iceland to study mechanical engineering right after highschool at 17.

My studies

I have a highschool diploma from le Lycée des petits champs in Paris, specialized in economy and mathematics. I have since then been working on getting a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering, being currently on my third year. In addition to that I was in a music school between the ages of 5 and 15, learning music theory and the saxophone.

Job experiences

I did two short internships in highschool. My first one in a sports newspaper (l'Equipe) where I learned about different press-related jobs. The second one was in a light manufacture called Loupi. I was mainly doing manual tasks, piecing together light sets, welding, etc. I got my first job after my first year at Háskóli Íslands, in a kindergarten, Leikskólinn Hraunborg. It helped me learn the language better and dealing with children. I worked there again during the next winter break. The next summer I started working in a warehouse for the company 1912, got my forklift certification. The job mainly consists in handling the forklift and preparing orders for restaurants, kitchens, schools, and various businesses. I currently work there two days a week.
